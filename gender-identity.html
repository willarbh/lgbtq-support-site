Your Child Is Transgender or Nonbinary

It’s common to feel overwhelmed when your child shares a different gender identity. Even supportive parents can struggle with this shift. But openness, communication, and learning together can strengthen your family bonds.

Helpful Terms to Know

  • Trans / Transgender: People whose gender identity differs from their sex assigned at birth, including trans men, trans women, and nonbinary individuals.
  • Nonbinary: A gender identity outside the categories of “man” or “woman.”
  • Gender Identity: One’s internal understanding of their gender.
  • Assigned Sex: The sex label given at birth based on physical anatomy.
  • Gender Expression: How a person outwardly expresses gender through clothes, voice, or behavior.
  • Gender Dysphoria: Distress from a mismatch between gender identity and assigned sex.
  • Gender Transition: The personal process of aligning outward identity with inner gender identity — this varies for each person.
  • Cisgender: When gender identity matches assigned sex.
  • Genderqueer / Gender Variant: People who challenge traditional gender categories.
  • Transphobia: Prejudice or fear toward trans and gender-diverse people.
  • Transfriendly: Spaces or people that affirm and support trans identities.
