Glossary of Terms

Term Definition
AllySomeone, often heterosexual or cisgender, who supports and advocates for LGBTQ+ rights.
Assigned SexThe classification as male, female, or intersex at birth based on anatomy.
AsexualA person who experiences little or no sexual attraction but may have romantic connections.
BigenderIdentifying with both male and female genders at different times or simultaneously.
BisexualAttraction to more than one gender.
Birth Sex / Biological SexPhysical and genetic traits categorizing male, female, or intersex at birth.
CisgenderGender identity matches assigned sex.
ClosetedNot having disclosed sexual orientation or gender identity.
Coming OutThe process of accepting and sharing one’s sexual orientation or gender identity.
GayTypically a man attracted to the same gender; also a general term for same-gender attraction.
Gender DysphoriaDistress from a mismatch between gender identity and assigned sex.
Gender ExpressionHow one outwardly shows gender.
Gender IdentityInternal sense of gender.
GenderqueerRejecting conventional gender categories.
Gender RoleSocietal expectations about behaviors and appearance based on gender.
Gender TransitionProcess of aligning outward identity with inner gender identity, including possible medical or legal changes.
Gender VariantDiffering from traditional gender expectations.
HeteronormativeAssuming heterosexuality as normal.
Heterosexual AllyStraight person supporting LGBTQ+ rights.
Heterosexual PrivilegeUnearned advantages straight people have.
HeterosexismDiscrimination favoring heterosexuality.
HomophobiaPrejudice against LGBTQ+ people.
IntersexBorn with physical sex traits that don’t fit typical definitions of male or female; a natural variation.
LesbianA woman attracted to other women.
LGBTQPAIAcronym for Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, Transgender, Queer/Questioning, Pansexual/Polyamorous, Asexual, and Intersex.
NonbinaryGender outside male/female categories.
OutBeing open about one’s LGBTQ+ identity.
OutingRevealing someone’s LGBTQ+ identity without permission.
PansexualAttraction regardless of gender.
QueerUmbrella term for identities outside heterosexual and cisgender norms; also a political stance.
QuestioningExploring or unsure about sexual orientation or gender identity.
Gender-affirming SurgeryMedical procedures aligning physical traits with gender identity.
Sexual OrientationPattern of romantic or sexual attraction.
StraightSlang for heterosexual.
Trans / TransgenderGender identity different from assigned sex.
TransfriendlySupportive and affirming of trans people.
TransphobiaFear or hatred of transgender people.
