Your Child Is Gay, Lesbian, or Bisexual

Hearing your child’s sexual orientation can bring up a mix of emotions — even if you’ve always supported LGBTQ+ rights. It’s normal to feel unsure about what to say or how to act. Remember: your child trusted you with something deeply personal. Your support now can make all the difference.

The Power of Support

Research shows LGBTQ+ youth with supportive familes experience:

  • Better mental health
  • Higher self-esteem
  • Stronger relationships at home
  • Greater success in school and later life

Many parents find this journey deepens honesty, love, and connection with their children.

Helpful Terms to Know

  • Gay: A person, often a man, emotionally, romantically, or sexually attracted to people of the same gender; also an umbrella term.
  • Lesbian: A woman emotionally, romantically, or sexually attracted to other women.
  • Bisexual: Attraction to more than one gender. This may include men, women, and nonbinary people.
  • Pansexual: Attraction to people regardless of gender identity.
  • Sexual Orientation: Who a person is emotionally, romantically, or sexually attracted to, separate from gender identity.
  • Coming Out: The ongoing process of recognizing and sharing one's sexual orientation.
  • Closeted: When someone has not publicly disclosed their sexual orientaiton.
  • Heteronormative: The assumption that heterosexual relationships are the default or "normal".
  • Heterosexism: The systme that privileges heterosexuality and marginalizes others.
